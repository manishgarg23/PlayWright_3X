## Section 1: Number Patterns

---

**Q1. What comes next in the series?**
```
2, 4, 8, 16, 32, ?
```
**Answer:** `64`
**Explanation:** Each number is multiplied by 2. (×2 pattern)

---

**Q2. What comes next?**
```
1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, ?
```
**Answer:** `21`
**Explanation:** Fibonacci sequence — each number = sum of previous two. (8 + 13 = 21)

---

**Q3. Find the missing number:**
```
3, 6, 11, 18, 27, ?
```
**Answer:** `38`
**Explanation:** Differences are +3, +5, +7, +9, +11 (odd numbers increasing). 27 + 11 = 38

---

**Q4. What is the missing number?**
```
100, 96, 88, 76, 60, ?
```
**Answer:** `40`
**Explanation:** Differences are -4, -8, -12, -16, -20 (decreasing by 4 each time). 60 - 20 = 40

---

**Q5. Complete the series:**
```
1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ?
```
**Answer:** `36`
**Explanation:** Perfect squares: 1², 2², 3², 4², 5², **6²** = 36

## Section 3: Logical Reasoning

---

**Q11. All roses are flowers. Some flowers fade quickly. Therefore:**
- A) All roses fade quickly
- B) Some roses fade quickly
- C) No roses fade quickly
- D) None of the above can be concluded

**Answer:** `D) None of the above can be concluded`
**Explanation:** "Some flowers fade quickly" doesn't tell us whether any of those flowers are roses. No conclusion about roses can be drawn.

---

**Q12. If ROAD is coded as URDG, how is SWAN coded?**

**Answer:** `VZDQ`
**Explanation:** Each letter is shifted +3 positions:
- S(19) → V(22)
- W(23) → Z(26)
- A(1)  → D(4)
- N(14) → Q(17)

---

**Q13. A man walks 5 km North, then 3 km East, then 5 km South. How far is he from his starting point?**
**Answer:** `3 km (East)`
**Explanation:** 5 km North and 5 km South cancel out. He is left 3 km East of his starting point.

---

**Q14. If you have a 3-litre jug and a 5-litre jug, how do you measure exactly 4 litres?**

**Answer:**
1. Fill the 5L jug
2. Pour from 5L into 3L until 3L is full → 5L now has 2L
3. Empty the 3L jug
4. Pour the 2L from 5L into 3L
5. Fill the 5L jug again
6. Pour from 5L into 3L until 3L is full (needs 1L) → 5L now has **4L**

---

**Q15. Which number should replace the question mark?**
```
| 4  | 6  |
| 8  | ?  |
| 16 | 24 |
```
**Answer:** `12`
**Explanation:** Each number in the right column = left column × 1.5. (4×1.5=6, 8×1.5=**12**, 16×1.5=24)

## Section 5: Mathematical Reasoning

---

**Q20. If 5 machines make 5 widgets in 5 minutes, how long does it take 100 machines to make 100 widgets?**
**Answer:** `5 minutes`
**Explanation:** Each machine makes 1 widget in 5 minutes. 100 machines working simultaneously each make 1 widget → 100 widgets total in 5 minutes.

---

**Q21. A bat and ball cost $1.10 together. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost?**
**Answer:** `$0.05 (5 cents)`
**Explanation:** Let ball = x. Bat = x + 1.00. Together: x + (x + 1.00) = 1.10 → 2x = 0.10 → x = **0.05**
*(Common wrong answer is $0.10 — that's the intuitive trap!)*

---

**Q22. How many times can you subtract 5 from 25?**
**Answer:** `Once`
**Explanation:** After the first subtraction, you have 20, not 25 anymore. You can only subtract 5 *from 25* once.

---

**Q23. A clock shows 3:15. What is the angle between the hour and minute hands?**
**Answer:** `7.5 degrees`
**Explanation:**
- Minute hand at 15 min = 90°
- Hour hand: at 3:00 it's at 90°, but by 3:15 it moves 0.5°/min × 15 = 7.5° extra → 97.5°
- Angle between them = 97.5° - 90° = **7.5°**

---

**Q24. What is the next number?**
```
0, 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, ?
```
**Answer:** `21`
**Explanation:** These are triangular numbers. Differences: +1, +2, +3, +4, +5, **+6** → 15 + 6 = 21

## Section 6: Visual / Spatial Reasoning

---

**Q25. How many squares are in a 4×4 grid?**
**Answer:** `30`
**Explanation:**
- 1×1 squares: 16
- 2×2 squares: 9
- 3×3 squares: 4
- 4×4 squares: 1
- Total: 16 + 9 + 4 + 1 = **30**

---

**Q26. A cube is painted red on all sides, then cut into 27 equal smaller cubes. How many small cubes have:**

| Painted Faces | Count |
|---|---|
| 3 red faces (corners) | 8 |
| 2 red faces (edges) | 12 |
| 1 red face (face center) | 6 |
| 0 red faces (inside) | 1 |
| **Total** | **27** |

---

**Q27. You look at a clock in a mirror. It shows 8:20. What is the actual time?**
**Answer:** `3:40`
**Explanation:** Mirror reverses left-right. The total clock face is 12 hours = 360°. Mirror time + actual time = 12:00. So actual = 12:00 - 8:20 = **3:40**
